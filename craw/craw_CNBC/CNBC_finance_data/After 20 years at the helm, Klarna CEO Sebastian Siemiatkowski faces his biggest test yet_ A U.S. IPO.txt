He now faces his biggest test yet with a U.S. IPO fast approaching.\nSiemiatkowski has grown Klarna into a fintech powerhouse and a brand that's virtually synonymous with the \"buy now, pay later\" payment method.\nHowever, his entrepreneurial journey hasn't been without challenges — and investors are likely scrutinize his track record in the leadup to Klarna's IPO.\nLONDON — After 20 years in the role as Klarna's CEO, Sebastian Siemiatkowski is about to face his toughest test yet as the financial technology firm prepares for its blockbuster debut in New York.\nSiemiatkowski, 43, co-founded Klarna in 2005 with fellow Swedish entrepreneurs Niklas Adalberth and Victor Jacobsson with the aim of taking on traditional banks and credit card firms with a more user-friendly online payments experience.\nToday, Klarna is synonymous with \"buy now, pay later\" — a method of payment that allows people to buy things and either defer payment until the end of the month or pay off their purchases over a series of equal, interest-free monthly installments.\nBut while Siemiatkowski has grown Klarna into a fintech powerhouse, his entrepreneurial journey hasn't been without its challenges — from facing rising competition from rivals such as PayPal, Affirm and Block's Afterpay, to an 85% valuation plunge.\nNevertheless, Siemiatkowski hasn't taken those challenges lying down and the outspoken co-founder isn't shy to challenge criticisms in the run up to an IPO that could value it at $15 billion."
